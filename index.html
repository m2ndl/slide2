Lingua Francas - The Starting Point

Definition (UNESCO 1953)
"A language used habitually by people whose mother tongues are different in order to facilitate communication between them"

Key characteristics:

  • Wide variation in speaker ability
  • Can be native, second, or foreign language
  • Examples: English, Arabic, Mandarin, Swahili
Critical question: What happens when people need to communicate but have limited access to the lingua franca?

Kiswahili - A Lingua Franca Example

From coast to continent

Native language: Coastal Kenya and Tanzania

North to Somalia, south to Mozambique

Spread as lingua franca:

  • Inland expansion through trade
  • Now used in education: Tanzania, Kenya, Uganda, Rwanda, Burundi
  • Political and public discourse throughout Great Lakes region
